Hsiang-Jen(Charles) Hong

I have finished my Ph.D. in Computer Science and Information Engineering at National Taiwan University of Science and Technology, Taiwan, at the beginning of this year. Now, I am serving the compulsory military service which will end on Feb. 2019. After finishing the services, my immediate career goal is to seek a postdoc opportunity in the USA.

During my Ph.D., I have worked on designing efficient algorithms for challenging assignment problems for grouping services. To deal with its combinatorial nature, we proposed some useful properties capturing the characteristic of an optimal solution. It thus lays a foundation for us to develop efficient algorithms. We also utilized some network flows techniques and pruning techniques based on dominance relations to further narrow down the search space of an optimal solution. We also proposed approximation algorithms to serve the need of grouping services with real-time requirement. I have also worked on developing algorithms for some query processing problems such as Top-k spatial keyword query and Top-k dominating query.

Recently, I work on a possible extension of patron assignment problems with a more general model. It might provide more flexibility in meeting different demands in different applications. For query processing, I have a collaboration with my previous lab member, Bagus Jati Santoso. We focus on designing efficient methods for answering a why-not k subscribers problem.

Ph.D. Dissertation

Optimal Patron Assignment Problems for Group Services:

With the increase of the system scale, the phenomenon of mutual aid among users will become more evident. In this dissertation, we denote the services that require summoning a collection of patrons to cooperatively fulfill a mission as ”group services”. The group services studied in this dissertation incorporate the lower and upper bound constraints of the participation number of each group into the group requirement. A group is successfully formed if and only if it satisfies the respective constraint. To facilitate group services, the profit model among the group services is a significant factor that should be considered. We propose two kinds of group services differentiated according to their profit models: profit-consistent group services, and rank-consistent group services. This dissertation focuses on assigning patrons to groups to best benefit the grouping system in an efficient manner.

Assignment Problem for Profit-Consistent Group Services

In profit-consistent group services, every patron participating in the same group contributes the same profit. We propose two approaches for the assignment problem. One aims at developing a 1/2-approximation algorithm. The other turns to seeking an exact algorithm. For this purpose, we introduce a theorem that captures a useful property of an optimal allocation. Based on this theorem, we design an efficient branch and bound algorithm to find an optimal solution.

[Approximation algorithm, branch-and-bound, exact algorithm]

Assignment Problem for Rank-Consistent Group Services:

 In rank-consistent group services, any patron assigned to the same group contributes different profit. However, the order of profits upon joining different groups is consistent among all patrons. By solving this challenging problem from the aspect of allocation vector, we can design efficient exact algorithms with much lower computation complexity by using novel techniques and additional pruning strategies based on the dominance relationships among allocation vectors.

[Approximation algorithm, branch-and-bound, dominance relations,  exact algorithm, hungarian method]
